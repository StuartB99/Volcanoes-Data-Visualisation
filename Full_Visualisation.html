Volcano Eruptions

Volcanoes have been around since the Earth was formed billions of years ago. They are ruptures in the crust of a planetary-mass object such as Earth. There are volcanoes everywhere, on every continent, even Antarctica. Volcanoes can be found on land as well as under the ocean.

Volcanoes are Earth's geologic architects. They have created more than 80 percent of our planet's surface, laying the foundation that has allowed life to thrive. Although most volcanoes are not active, there is still quite a few that has erupted in the past 20 years.

Not all volcanoes are the same. There are various different types of volcanoes. The most common one is the stratovolcano which is a tall, conical volcano composed of one layer of hardened lava, tephra, and volcanic ash. These volcanoes are characterized by a steep profile and periodic, explosive eruptions.

Volcanoes have eruptions which is when they spew hot lava, volcanic ash and gases from a magma chamber below the surface of the planet. These eruptions crafts mountains as well as create craters.

To measure the relative explosiveness of volcanoes, the Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) is used, ranging from 0 to 7. Devised in 1982, the volume of products, eruption cloud height, and qualitative observations are used to determine the explosivity value.

Each volcano eruption happens differently with different events occuring. However most burst into life in explosive eruptions and emit ash. Although lava is often synonymous with volcanoes, they only occur 29.7% of times. An unexcepted side effect of volcano eruptions are earthquakes which occur together with eruptions 16.5% of times.

Another surprising information, that is often in contrast to what people believe, is that eruptions that have loud audible noises are rare, occuring only 3.75% of times.

Volcano eruptions does not happen consistently across different places. Some countries are more susceptible to volcano eruptions than others whereas some may not have volcanoes at all.

The more eruption prone countries are New Zealand, Iceland, Italy and Japan among others. Most of Africa and the Middle East do not have volcanic eruptions.
